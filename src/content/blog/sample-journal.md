Some days the listening takes you somewhere unexpected. I put on City and Colour during a lunch break — comfortable, familiar territory — and by 4 PM I was deep into Icelandic ambient music I'd never heard before.

The path was something like: acoustic folk → Bon Iver → experimental production → Ólafur Arnalds → whatever Spotify decided to queue next. That last step is where the algorithm actually earns its keep — not when it feeds you more of the same, but when it catches you in a mood and pushes you somewhere adjacent.

The Arnalds record I landed on was particularly interesting. Piano and electronics woven together so tightly you stop trying to separate them. It's the kind of music that changes the texture of your afternoon without demanding your attention.

Filed under: things I never would have found if I'd just hit play on the same playlist again.
