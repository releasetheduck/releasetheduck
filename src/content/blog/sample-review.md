There's something about starting the week with a record that demands your full attention. *Lament* is that kind of album — the one that grabs you by the collar in the first thirty seconds and doesn't let go until the final note rings out.

What makes this record special isn't just the intensity. It's how Bolm navigates between raw screams and moments of genuine vulnerability. The production walks a tightrope between polished and raw, landing in a sweet spot that serves both the melodic hooks and the hardcore foundations.

The guitar work here deserves special mention. There are riffs that would feel at home on a punk record from 2003, sitting right next to post-rock textures that wouldn't be out of place on a Mogwai album.

## Standout Tracks

The opening track sets the tone perfectly. By the time you reach the mid-album stretch, you're already committed. The closing sequence is where the record truly earns its replay value — each listen reveals another layer you missed before.

This is hardcore for people who want to *feel* something, not just hear something loud.
