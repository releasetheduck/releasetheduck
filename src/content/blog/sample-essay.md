There's a difference between hearing music and listening to it. We all know this intuitively, but it's worth examining what happens when you start writing about what you hear.

Before I started this journal, I'd consume albums like content — background noise while working, a soundtrack to the commute, something to fill the silence. The music was always there, but I wasn't always *with* it.

Writing about an album forces a different kind of engagement. You can't describe what you heard if you weren't paying attention. You can't articulate why a particular chord progression moved you if you were scrolling through your phone when it happened.

The practice doesn't have to be formal. A few sentences about what stood out. A note about where you were when you heard it. Even just naming the feeling — that's enough to shift from passive consumption to active listening.

Three months in, I'm hearing things in familiar records I never noticed before. Not because the music changed, but because I did.
